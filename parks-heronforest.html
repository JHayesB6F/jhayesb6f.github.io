Reconnect with nature


A calming, small nature reserve tucked away along a side path; Heron Forest is the stuff of dreams. The most noise pollution you'll get here is bird chirping and swaying branches in the breeze. Heron Forest was home to an old country manor for centuries, but it became dilapidated after years of neglect.

Nowadays, Heron Forest is a nature reserve full of birdlife and red squirrels, one of few sites outside London that isn't privately owned. We don't have a café here or any refreshments, but that is to help preserve the peace in the forest and instill a sense of calm.

When walking around, look out for the ruins of the manor garden to the southeast, and the stairs leading down to the main garden - perfect for stunning photos!

We ask that you avoid bringing disruptive pets here, as we want to protect this fragile ecosystem from damage, and keep the site open for years to come.

Before you go

Facilities

Heron Forest is a smaller size nature reserve, and as such does not have many facilities available. Car parking is very limited in the area, and resident permits are needed. Permanent toilets are not yet available, but we currently have portable toilets installed by the entrance and are building a permanent facility later this year.

Location

Heron Forest is located on Wold Lane, BR5 3AG. The No. 11 and the No. 73 buses both stop outside the main entrance, about a 300m walk. The entrance is flat, but unpaved, and is not lit after dark.   

Opening times

The forest is open to the public from 7:00 until sunset daily. To stay longer, speak to the forest manager by the entrance. The forest is not open during heavy rainfall and is off-limits to pets during mating season

Advisories

As this is a nature reserve and not a conventional park, most paths will be unsuitable for those with reduced mobility. We are constructing disabled-freindly paths over the coming months, but in the meantime please use the marked paths only.
