Essex's largest melting pot


Brick Lane is home to the largest immigrant population in Essex, beating large cities like Chelmsford and Colchester. The diversity of our residents is reflected in the unique culture instilled in Brick Lane, shaped by generations of families. Close to the river estuary, we have recieved millions of tons of produce since the 18th century, and plenty of workers arriving also from mainland Europe. Brookstead was designated a Special Economic Zone, meaning that all trade within the town limits was exempt for taxes, driving up the economy. However, since the considerable floods in the 1950s, investors have left in search of larger areas like London.

Although, not many residents left and continued to stay in Brookstead. To support the residents, the local council allowed for development of small businesses along Brick Lane, excluding chain companies from operating there; this has been pivotal in its success story. Wandering the street gives you access to different cultures around the world, from Eastern European to Middle Eastern, African to South Asian; there's more than enough choice!

Besides the wide array of food choices, there are other activites you can try whilst here; for example, you can hire a bicycle for £1/hour and ride alongside the sports courts watching people play football; visit one of many art galleries, or visit the temple to the south. Be sure to visit soon!

Before you go

Facilities

Brick Lane is in the centre of town and is mostly comprised of commerical buildings. Chain stores such as Superdrug and Tesco Express are located on Elm Street, to the southeast. There are some convinience stores near the road junctions that sell refreshments and snacks at good prices; toilets are available at the junction with North Street, in Glades Shopping Centre, and at Station Plaza.

Location

Brick Lane is 450m from Station Plaza, and 200m from the Bus Station. Directions to Brick Lane are well-signposted, by the brick icon on street signs. If driving, please park in the Glades Shopping Centre car park, as on-street parking is permit-only 24/7.

Opening times

Shops are generally open from 7:00-21:00. Restaurant dinner service is normally from 17:00 until late. If you are staying late, please be quiet when you leave to avoid disturbing our neighbours!

Advisories

Brick Lane gets very busy on the weekends and during the school holidays. If using a wheelchair or have sensory challenges, try visiting at a quieter time instead.
