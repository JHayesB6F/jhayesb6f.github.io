Accessibility

An Accessible Brookstead


Not all of our visitors are able-bodied, so we want to accomodate as many needs as possible for all that visit Brookstead. This webpage will show what services and features we have available for those with visual or auditory impairments, and for visitors coming to the town for the first time with disabilities.

Features of the website

For hard of hearing

Videos on this website do not have any spoken lines, just ambient audio from the streets. You can connect a pair of headphones or use a Bluetooth connection through a hearing aid to hear the videos. Where necessary, closed captions will be provided.

For persons with visual impairments

This website is designed to be lightweight and simple with minimal plugins. For this reason, most of the website should be compatible with screen readers and assistive features

For persons with motor difficulties

Most of this website should be accessible only using a keyboard. For assistive devices such as head tracking or specialist input devices, use the scroll feature as normal. Some videos may be incompatible with these methods, so send us a message if you need assistance

Feedback

Please let us know if there are any accessible features you would like to add to this website, or if there are any problems you want to be addressed. Your email will not be stored, as no email will actually be sent.

Before you visit

Terrain

Brookstead is mostly flat terrain, as it is on a river estuary. There are some large hills on the outskirts of town that may be too steep, and some stairs in town can be hard to climb. We are currently adding disabled ramps and shallower stairs to most of our steep routes, but in other cases please follow the marked alternative route.

Attractions

All of our featured attractions are disabled-freindly with accessible routes throughout and few stairs. Due to the weather, we may not be able to provide outdoors accessible routes and instead ask that you use the indoors tour guide instead. Wheelchair parking is available, but our attractions cannot be held liable for any losses or damage   

Getting here

Road: Signs to Brookstead are from the A132 (Burnham Rd). From London, follow signs to A12 (M25) and turn onto the A127 at Gallows Corner. From there, stay on the road until the Wickford exit. From Essex, follow signs to A12 (Chelmsford) and leave at J17 (A130). Parking in the town centre is expensive and limited due to many buildings having listed status; please use the Park & Ride facilities near Fambridge instead.      

Train: The closest railway station is Althorne, on the Southminster line. Take any train to Southend Victoria and change at Wickford. Althorne has a very steep approach road that is often slippery. Please use North Fambridge instead when the Park & Ride shuttle is operating. We are looking to get direct trains from Shenfield to a purpose-built station starting Summer 2024.                           

Boat: Our marina is to the north of the town centre with regular ferry services to Tolleshunt, Maldon and Mersea. This is subject to weather conditions, and may be cancelled at short notice, so check before you travel.
